Cristiano Ronaldo Named Global Ambassador for Esports World Cup 2025

In a landmark announcement that bridges the gap between traditional sports and digital competition, the Esports World Cup Foundation (EWCF) has revealed global football icon Cristiano Ronaldo as the official Global Ambassador for the Esports World Cup 2025. This strategic partnership signals a major shift in the evolving landscape of sports and gaming, uniting two powerful global communities—esports fans and football enthusiasts. As one of the most recognized athletes in history, Ronaldo’s influence is expected to elevate the tournament's visibility and inspire the next generation of competitive gamers around the world.

A Powerful Collaboration: Sports Meets Esports

Cristiano Ronaldo’s appointment as the face of the Esports World Cup is not just a branding move—it is a signal that esports is becoming a mainstream global entertainment force. With this partnership, the Esports World Cup Foundation aims to extend its reach far beyond the traditional gaming audience and attract fans of global sports, especially football. The campaign theme for the 2025 edition, “Rise Above,” aligns perfectly with Ronaldo’s own story of perseverance, discipline, and greatness. As part of his ambassadorial role, Ronaldo will lead the global promotion of the tournament, amplifying the message of rising above challenges—both in the game and in life.

Ronaldo on Esports: A New Frontier of Sport

Cristiano Ronaldo, a five-time Ballon d'Or winner and the highest goal-scorer in the history of football, had this to say about his new role:
“Sport is always evolving and esports is one more frontier. The commitment, drive, talent, and intensity I have been able to see in esports athletes is no different from what I’ve experienced on the pitch. I’m proud to join the Esports World Cup as Global Ambassador—standing with esports athletes who rise above and inspire the next generation.”
His words underscore the growing respect for esports professionals, whose daily grind, training, and dedication now rival that of traditional athletes. For Ronaldo to recognize this parity is a huge validation for the gaming world.

Global Reach: Why Ronaldo is the Perfect Fit

Cristiano Ronaldo isn’t just an athlete; he’s a global brand. With over 650 million followers on Instagram, 115 million on X (formerly Twitter), and 75 million YouTube subscribers, he is the most-followed individual across social platforms worldwide. This unparalleled digital presence makes Ronaldo an ideal ambassador for the Esports World Cup, especially as the event aims to attract new audiences and markets.

According to EWCF CEO Ralf Reichert:
“Cristiano Ronaldo connects generations—from lifelong football fans to today’s emerging competitors. His pursuit of greatness mirrors the spirit of the Esports World Cup: the biggest stage for the best players. We’re humbled to have the greatest of all time stand with us in this moment for esports.”

Not Ronaldo’s First Step into Esports

While this announcement may seem surprising to some, it isn’t Ronaldo’s first interaction with the world of esports. In 2023, he worked with the EWCF to help launch the Esports World Cup 2024, and even made an appearance at its Closing Ceremony in Riyadh, further reinforcing his support for the industry.

Although his participation in the 2025 edition in person is yet to be confirmed, his continued involvement highlights his growing interest in the esports ecosystem and his commitment to supporting digital athletes and the global gaming community.

Athletes and Esports: A Growing Trend

Cristiano Ronaldo is not the only legendary athlete venturing into the esports space. Over the past few years, several high-profile sports figures have forged similar paths:

  • In 2023, Lionel Messi became co-owner of KRU Esports, a rising Latin American gaming organization.
  • Earlier in 2025, Team Gullit, an academy founded by Dutch football legend Ruud Gullit, was acquired by global gaming powerhouse Team Liquid.
  • Numerous NBA, NFL, and cricket stars have also invested in esports teams or participated in gaming tournaments, showcasing how deeply intertwined the worlds of sports and gaming have become.
These moves highlight a shared belief: competitive gaming is no longer just a pastime—it's a professional, lucrative, and globally celebrated career path.

What the Esports World Cup 2025 Means for the Industry

The Esports World Cup 2025 is set to be the biggest esports event in history, both in scale and prize money. Hosted in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, the tournament will run across several weeks and feature top games, global teams, and the world’s best players competing for a $70 million prize pool—the largest in esports history.

By involving a sports personality of Ronaldo’s stature, EWCF is not only increasing visibility but also strengthening the legitimacy of esports as a recognized professional sport.

Conclusion: A Defining Moment for Esports

Cristiano Ronaldo’s appointment as the Global Ambassador of the Esports World Cup 2025 marks a monumental moment in the evolution of competitive gaming. It is a clear sign that esports is entering a new era—one where traditional and digital sports are no longer separate, but part of the same global ecosystem of entertainment and competition.

With a message of inspiration and excellence, Ronaldo’s campaign theme “Rise Above” serves as a call to action for gamers around the world to pursue greatness, break boundaries, and rise to new heights—both in the virtual arena and in real life.

As the Esports World Cup 2025 draws closer, one thing is certain: with Cristiano Ronaldo leading the charge, this tournament is going to make history.
